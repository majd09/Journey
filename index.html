The Czech Republic also known as Czechia is a landlocked country in Central Europe. Historically known as Bohemia it is bordered by Austria to the south, Germany to the west, Poland to the northeast, and Slovakia to the southeast. The Czech Republic has a hilly landscape that covers an area of 78,871 square kilometers (30,452 sq mi) with a mostly temperate continental and oceanic climate. The capital and largest city is Prague; other major cities and urban areas include Brno, Ostrava, Plzeň and Liberec.

What Is Our History

Český Krumlov is a city in the Czech Republic's South Bohemia region. The Vltava River divides it in half, and its 13th-century castle is its main feature. The castle features an 11-hectare garden, an original 17th-century baroque theater, and elements of Gothic, Renaissance, and baroque architecture. From the top of its circular belltower, there are expansive views of the river and the old town.

Český Krumlov Castle

Český Krumlov Castle is one of the most important monuments in Central Europe in terms of architectural level, cultural tradition and scale. Perhaps due to hidden position in a remote corner of South Bohemia. Its building development has been preserved with the original layout, material structure, interior installations and architectural details from the 14th to the 19th century.
